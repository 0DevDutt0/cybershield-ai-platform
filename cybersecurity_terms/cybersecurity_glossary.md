# Cybersecurity Terms and Definitions

## A

### Access Control
A security technique that restricts access to resources based on user identity, roles, or permissions.

### Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)
A prolonged and targeted computer intrusion, typically carried out by well-funded organizations or nation-states.

### Attack Surface
The sum of all potential entry points an attacker could exploit to gain access to a system or network.

## B

### Backup
A copy of data or system configurations that can be used to restore information in case of data loss or system failure.

### Botnet
A network of compromised computers (bots) controlled by a malicious actor to perform coordinated attacks.

### Brute Force Attack
An attack that attempts to gain access to a system by trying many possible password combinations.

## C

### Cipher
An algorithm used for encrypting and decrypting data.

### Cloud Security
The practice of protecting data, applications, and infrastructure in cloud computing environments.

### Cryptography
The practice and study of secure communication techniques that prevent third parties from reading private information.

### Cyber Attack
An attack on computer systems, networks, or digital devices with the intent to steal data, damage systems, or disrupt operations.

### Cybersecurity
The protection of internet-connected systems, including hardware, software, and data, from cyber threats.

## D

### Data Breach
An incident where sensitive, protected, or confidential data is accessed or disclosed without authorization.

### Denial of Service (DoS)
An attack that makes a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users.

### Digital Signature
A mathematical technique used to validate the authenticity and integrity of digital messages or documents.

## E

### Encryption
The process of encoding information so that only authorized parties can access it.

### Endpoint Security
Protection of end-user devices (laptops, smartphones, tablets) from security threats.

### Ethical Hacking
Authorized testing of computer systems, networks, or applications to identify vulnerabilities.

## F

### Firewall
A network security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules.

## I

### Identity and Access Management (IAM)
The security discipline that ensures the right individuals have the appropriate access to technology resources.

### Incident Response
The process of managing the aftermath of a security breach or cyber attack.

### Intrusion Detection System (IDS)
A system that monitors network traffic for suspicious activity and issues alerts when such activity is detected.

## K

### Keylogger
A type of surveillance software that records keystrokes on a computer or mobile device.

## M

### Malware
Any software designed to harm or exploit any computer, computer system, or computer network.

### Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
An authentication method that requires users to provide two or more different types of evidence to verify their identity.

## N

### Network Security
The protection of network infrastructure and data from unauthorized access, misuse, malfunction, modification, destruction, or improper disclosure.

### NIST
National Institute of Standards and Technology - a U.S. government agency that develops cybersecurity standards and guidelines.

## P

### Phishing
A type of social engineering attack that attempts to steal sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity in electronic communications.

### Privilege Escalation
The act of exploiting a vulnerability to gain higher-level permissions than originally granted.

### Protection
Security measures taken to prevent unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of information assets.

## R

### Remote Access
The ability to access and control a computer or network from a location other than the physical location of the device.

### Risk Assessment
The process of identifying and evaluating potential threats and vulnerabilities that could impact an organization's assets.

### Ransomware
A type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment for the decryption key.

## S

### Security Incident
An event that could potentially compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information or systems.

### Security Patch
A piece of software designed to update and fix security vulnerabilities in computer systems.

### Secure Sockets Layer (SSL)
A cryptographic protocol that provides secure communication over a computer network.

### Social Engineering
The manipulation of individuals to divulge confidential information.

### Spam
Unwanted, unsolicited electronic messages sent in bulk.

## T

### Threat Actor
An individual or group that poses a threat to an organization's information assets.

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
An authentication method that requires users to provide two different types of evidence to verify their identity.

## V

### Vulnerability
A weakness in a system that could be exploited by a threat actor.

## W

### Web Application Firewall (WAF)
A security solution that monitors and filters HTTP traffic between a web application and the Internet.

### Whaling
A phishing attack that targets high-profile individuals or executives within an organization.

### Wireless Security
Protection of wireless networks and devices from unauthorized access and attacks.

## X

### XSS (Cross-Site Scripting)
A type of injection attack that enables attackers to execute scripts in the victim's browser.

## Z

### Zero Trust Security
A security model that assumes no implicit trust and verifies every user and device attempting to access resources.
